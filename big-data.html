Thinking Outside the Browse and Search Box: Big Data as a Complement to Navigation Design

A published magazine article in UXPA Mag's Cloudworking and Big Data issue.

My Roles

  • Author

Read My Article

In too many website redesigns I've worked on, clients are chomping at the bit to get to the navigation design phase.

"What kind of menus will we have? How many levels? Is there going to be hamburger?"

In reality, I've shared, most visitors aren't accessing content by mastering mega menus or perusing popups.

Data analytics and usability studies consistently show that most visitors actually arrive through a 3rd party search engine, or through some piece of related content or page already on the site.

Through harnessing the power of taxonomy and access trends, I wrote about a myriad of different design techniques for making your content findable without even having to click on a menu button.
