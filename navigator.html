FirstCell Navigator

Designing an enterprise business application to facilitate capture of new sales leads and track B2B customer orders for cell phone service and device procurement.

My Roles

  • Lead UX Designer

The project: Design a workflow enterprise business application from scratch (no Salesforce widgets to lean on here) to help telecom employees deliver service to new customers – and also capture new sales leads.

The challenge: Deliver a high-information density CRM experience – typically exclusive to call center-style widescreen monitor viewports only – for sales agents and employees who were out in the field a couple times a year on a mobile device, still needing to view long lists of work items and customer records down to the most granular data element.

Desktop was priority, but I still had to make scalable design decisions early on in consideration of an eventual mobile experience ask from the client.

My role was focused on interaction design – I had to create the navigation model, organize information display, choreograph transitions between actions, and create highly interactive wireframe prototypes to simulate the experience end to end. Given the emphasis on prototyping for this project, I used Axure RP to build all clickable wireframe prototypes.

In a design team of two, I worked with a visual designer who delivered pixel-perfect mockups of select wireframe prototype screens, focused on precision aesthetic, typography, space, and color.

My wireframe prototypes for navigating long lists of work items:

To support desktop employees needing to rapidly scan the entire worklist while simultaneously inspecting details for a single work item, I used a split vertical layout. I made sure to distinguish between new work items and old work items to better highlight potentially urgent work.

In the linked desktop wireframe prototype, I created clickable interactions to depict loading a work item into the detail view pane, pulling down filters, opening task detail modals, marking tasks as complete, adding comments, and adding custom tasks.

The resultant visually-styled mockup (credit the talented visual designer) elevates the experience with production-ready attention to fonts, shadows, padding, and color.

My multi-step form as a wireframe prototype for capturing new customer leads:

Visual designer's fully-styled mockup pass:

In the context of a business to business telecom sales lead, there are many dozens of pieces of information needed for collection during the capture process. Knowing that studies show how forms are more likely to be completed – and accurately – when the form is broken up into multiple steps, I designed a scalable multi-step form navigation model with corresponding design patterns for all needed input types.

In the linked wireframe prototype above, I prototyped navigation through the form, and made all input fields interactive to convey edit states and illustrate dynamic save feedback.

In this linked prototype page, I demonstrate the interaction for a conditional field array using progressive disclosure.

My admin interface wireframe prototype for users to create custom business rules:

Finally, in the spirit of a CMS and/or custom business rules engine, I designed an admin interface for building and configuring the individual tasks and rules that govern work items. This experience relies heavily on the use of modal windows to manage embedded forms without overwhelming the user with a copious number of fields from the outset.
