Awards and Compensation Tool

A from-scratch enterprise workflow tool. Created to better facilitate employee recognition, and to increase transparency and equity in compensation.

My Roles

  • UX design lead
  • Usability lead

My interactive prototype of the end-to-end product

The project: Help an organization better manage annual bonus amounts. Bring more equity, transparency, and business intelligence to the annual bonus determination experience. And do so by departing from the archaic process of managing bonuses through offline Excel spreadsheets and manual version control.

After conducting user research with managers at the client organization (the users who were most in need of a UI for creating and submitting award nominations), I created screenflow diagrams to validate the workflow and breadth of interface touchpoints.

Despite the temptation by some stakeholders to recreate Excel in a web interface, I demonstrated through usability testing the efficacy of card-based design for managing workflow.


Rather than attempt to jam all the information about everything into a single screen, I designed slide-out panels to give users information on-demand in the right place at the right time only.

I enjoyed the challenge of designing for a product that lacked much by way of tangible imagery or photos to support the rather abstract financial content. Using carefully planned interface elements - like the subtley of cards, shadows, type hierarchy, and an occassional line icon - the experience was well received in usability testing, and successfully departed from the tropes of table-based workflow systems.

In another example of strides I made in moving away from a plain-text numeric entry experience, I prototyped interactive sliders to facilitate numeric data entry - a way to bound a user's input range without inelegant text value validation.

I took most satisfaction in transforming a stereotypical long government form into a guided multi-step wizard - supported by high success rates in the task-based usability testing I conducted.
